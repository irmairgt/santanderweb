Raqueros

Los Raqueros de Santander is a statue located in the city of Santander, Spain, near the Muelle de la Escuela de Pesca. The sculpture, created by artist Manuel García and unveiled in 2000, represents a group of boys known as "raqueros," who, in the 19th and early 20th centuries, would dive into the bay to retrieve coins and other objects thrown by passengers on ferries. The boys were part of the city's working-class maritime culture, risking their lives in the cold waters to earn a living. The statue captures this historical practice and symbolizes the resilience and spirit of the people of Santander.


Today, Los Raqueros has become an iconic landmark, celebrated for its connection to the city’s maritime heritage and its portrayal of the city's history. It is a popular spot for both locals and tourists.
