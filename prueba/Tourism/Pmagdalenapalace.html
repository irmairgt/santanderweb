Magdalena Palace

The Palacio de la Magdalena is a historic building located on the La Magdalena Peninsula in Santander, Cantabria, Spain. Built between 1909 and 1911 by architect Leonardo Rucabado, it features a neoclassical style with English and French influences. Originally, it was designed as a summer residence for the Spanish Royal Family, especially for King Alfonso XIII. In 1977, the palace became property of the Santander City Council and is now used as a venue for cultural events, congresses, and conferences. It also hosts the summer courses of the Menéndez Pelayo International University (UIMP).


The building is known for its elegant architecture, with towers, stone columns, and an interior decorated with period furniture. The palace’s gardens offer spectacular views of the sea and the city of Santander. It is one of the most popular landmarks in the city and a symbol of its cultural and historical heritage.
