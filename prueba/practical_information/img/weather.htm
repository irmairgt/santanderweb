Weather in January

In January, Santander residents can expect a cool start to the year, with the chilly temperature prevailing from winter months. With the lowest sea temperature of 12.9°C (55.2°F), the waters of Santander are not inviting for swimming. Possibility of snowfalls, with an average of 4mm (0.16") snow and 0.4 snowfall days, January shows the winter aspect of Santander. Political winds blow harder in January with an average speed of 19km/h (11.8mph), reaching up to a maximum of 20.2km/h (12.6mph). It is advised to pack warm clothes for outdoor activities and not to forget about rain gear.

Temperature

Santander sees its lowest temperatures during January and February, with an average high-temperature of 11.8°C (53.2°F).

Humidity

In January, the average relative humidity is 74%.

Rainfall

In Santander, during January, the rain falls for 13.8 days and regularly aggregates up to 41mm (1.61") of precipitation. In Santander, during the entire year, the rain falls for 133.9 days and collects up to 336mm (13.23") of precipitation.

Snowfall

January, February and December are months with snowfall. The months with the most snowfall in Santander are January and February, with an average 4mm (0.16") of accumulated snow.

Sea temperature

In January, the average sea temperature is 12.9°C (55.2°F).
Note: Immersing oneself in 12.9°C (55.2°F) at sub-10°C (14°F) temperatures is a recipe for harsh cold shock and quickened breathing unless adequately equipped.

Daylight

In January, the average length of the day in Santander is 9h and 23min.
On the first day of the month, sunrise is at 08:47 and sunset at 17:49. On the last day of January, sunrise is at 08:32 and sunset at 18:24 CET.

Sunshine

The average sunshine in January in Santander is 5.8h.

UV index

The months with the lowest UV index in Santander are January, February, April and November, with an average maximum UV index of 3. A UV Index of 3 to 5 symbolizes a medium vulnerability from unprotected exposure to Sun's UV rays for the average person.
Note: The average daily UV index of 3 in January transforms into the following instructions:
Put precautions into practice - Guidance to elude sunburn is provided. The solar radiation is most powerful near the mid-day, so the exposure to the direct Sun should be reduced accordingly. For effective sun eye protection, rely on sunglasses certified for UVA and UVB resistance. Take heed! The reflection of snow can intensify the Sun's UV radiation nearly double.
